# Price Comparison

To make sure we are getting the most bang for the buck, I created a [price comparison table](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1kJ4AdCbZYQlSQ2yOezcn3ApZPejCnOpOeinDMrVkBYM). It lists the main parts (like ADC, DAC, coarse approach) for this and other DIY SPM project. By now I didn't include AFMs, because they are so different from my design that there's basically no comparison of parts.

Here's a nice, and probably outdated at the time you are reading, graph of prices. The up to date one is in the spreadsheet itself.

![Chart showing the stacked prices of each main part of various DIY SPMs](/assets/price-comparison-chart.svg)

Note that some projects are indicated with "Not all the parts". It may be because they are not publicly specified, the project is already too expensive, or just because I didn't bother searching enough.

I also want to point out that the graph alone makes the project of Philip Turner not seem as competitive, but he is going for a super advanced design with virtually no piezo distortions and compatible with ultra high vacuum, that may futurelly allow manufacturing at the nanoscale. It's very cool to [watch it unfold](https://x.com/philipturnerar), and it's extensively documented ([here](https://github.com/philipturner/capacitive-displacement-metrology) and [here](https://github.com/philipturner/transimpedance-amplifier)).
